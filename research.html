  • Dynamic Mechanism for Return Incentives in Asylum Systems
    Work in Progress Bachelor Honours Research · with Prof. Robert Dur — Erasmus University Rotterdam, Aug 2025 – Present
    Developing a sequential screening model of return payments for asylum seekers. Extending time-inconsistency frameworks to analyze how credibility constraints shape migration decisions.
  • Optimal Taxation of E-Cigarette Products
    Work in Progress Research Project · with Prof. Hans van Kippersluis — Erasmus University Rotterdam, Jun 2025 – Present
    Building a welfare-based model to determine the optimal e-cigarette excise tax for the Netherlands. Incorporating cross-border arbitrage and estimating Dutch-specific elasticities through spatial variation analysis.
  • Poll Weighting Effects in Intra-Party Primaries on Electoral Strategy and Outcomes
    Completed Undergraduate Research Program · with Prof. Rhee Keeyoung — Sungkyunkwan University, Feb 2025
    Developed a game-theoretic model for intra-party primaries, analyzing candidate positioning across configurations. Simulated equilibria to characterize how poll weighting and constraints moderate political extremism.
  • Gender-Specific Cardiovascular Screening Intervention Design
    Completed Research Project · with Prof. Hans van Kippersluis — Erasmus University Rotterdam, Feb 2025
    Conducted a medical literature review on cardiovascular disease and preventive programs. Constructed a disease–age–gender panel from CBS data for Zuid-Holland municipalities. Designed an intervention to address gender-specific attitudes and increase early screening uptake. Presented findings at a symposium to clinicians, data scientists, and insurance representatives.
  • Effects of Childbirth Incentives by Local Governments on Birth Rate
    Completed Undergraduate Research Program · with Prof. Lee Dongwon — Sungkyunkwan University, Aug 2024
    Constructed and analyzed a South Korean municipal panel dataset on childbirth incentives and demographic outcomes. Estimated the causal impact of financial incentives for first- and second-born children on fertility rates.
  • Numerical Investigation for Economic Modeling Using Python
    Completed Undergraduate Research Program · with Prof. Lee Junsang — Sungkyunkwan University, Jul 2022
    Modeled AD-AS dynamics in Python to analyze output under fiscal and monetary shocks. Simulated effects on equilibrium stability and inflation-output tradeoffs using dynamic visualization tools.
